Inclusive Stock Photos & Illustrations

2 min read • May 5, 2020

From self-isolation in a small Vancouver apartment I watch communities across the globe gather and stand up against the systemic racism that, somehow, still plagues our world. I’m upset, angry, hurt, and, perhaps most of all, confused.

It’s imperative for us to reconsider our personal and professional practices and recognize the actions that we can all take to make a difference.

During the past few weeks I have been working with a small team to build out a new website for Sage Foundation, a non-profit dedicated to supporting and celebrating underrepresented communities in Vancouver. On several occasions throughout this project, I have been stumped by the lack of inclusive stock photographs and imagery available by many of the most popular services. I have compiled some of the better resources that I have found into the list below. If you are aware of any others please let me know as I would love to keep adding more to the list.
